Research Vision

I study the intersection of geospatial intelligence and human behavior — building computational frameworks that decode how people move through, interact with, and shape urban environments.

My work bridges satellite remote sensing, mobility analytics, and machine learning to create spatial systems that understand cities the way humans experience them.

⚙

Current Work

At Nagoya University's UCLab, my research focuses on developing multimodal approaches to urban intelligence:

  • Human mobility-aware embeddings for urban spatial analysis
  • Multimodal fusion systems integrating satellite imagery, POI data, and social media signals
  • Urban site suitability modeling for intelligent city planning
  • Behavioral data-driven frameworks for sustainable urban design
⚛

Technical Focus

The technical core of my research lies in building systems that fuse heterogeneous spatial data sources — satellite imagery at multiple resolutions, human mobility traces, points of interest, and social media geo-tagged content — into unified representations that enable downstream urban intelligence tasks.
